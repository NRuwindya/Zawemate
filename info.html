Simple Budgeting

Budgeting is the first step to financial control. It helps you understand where your money comes from, where it goes, and how you can use it better. Without a plan, it’s easy to spend more than you earn and end up stressed about money.

How to Start:

  • Write down all sources of income (salary, freelance, side hustles, etc.).
  • List your expenses: rent, food, transport, bills, shopping, and entertainment.
  • Divide expenses into needs (must-haves) and wants (nice-to-haves).
  • Use the 50/30/20 rule: 50% on needs, 30% on wants, 20% on savings or debt repayment.
  • Review your budget at the end of each month and adjust as your income or priorities change.

Smart Saving

Saving is about preparing for tomorrow while living comfortably today. Even small amounts saved regularly can grow into big amounts over time. Many people struggle because they wait until the end of the month to save—by then, the money is already gone.

Practical Saving Tips:

  • Pay yourself first: move money into savings as soon as you get paid.
  • Start with 10% of your income. If that feels too much, begin with 5% and increase slowly.
  • Open a separate savings account so you’re not tempted to spend.
  • Build an emergency fund with at least 3–6 months of living expenses.
  • Save for specific goals like travel, education, or a new gadget to stay motivated.
